About the Lab

Where acoustics
meets architecture

SONORIS Lab — from the Latin sonoris, meaning "resonant" — brings together acoustics, psychoacoustics, soundscape research, and architectural systems under a cohesive interdisciplinary identity.

Based at the NUS Department of Architecture, we advance understanding of how sound shapes human experience in high-density urban environments. Our work spans fundamental perception science, environmental quality assessment, and design translation — bridging laboratory findings with built outcomes.

We engage strategically with national R&D priorities under Singapore's City of Tomorrow, Education Research Framework, and Green Built Environment initiatives, working with agencies including HDB, MOE, NRF, and MND.

Acoustics & Psychoacoustics

Rigorous investigation of how people perceive, evaluate, and respond to sound in architectural and urban contexts.

Soundscape Design

Human-centric frameworks for shaping auditory environments that support health, comfort, and community.

Architectural Systems

Material, spatial, and technical strategies that translate acoustic evidence into design practice and policy.

Research Training

A strong platform for graduate students and early-career researchers to develop internationally competitive expertise.

Research Focus Areas

Six themes,
one resonant mission

01

Psychoacoustic Perception

How humans perceive loudness, tonality, roughness and sharpness — and how these perceptions vary across cultures and urban contexts.

02

Urban Soundscape Quality

Assessing and designing the sonic character of parks, streets, housing estates and transit environments using ISO 12913 frameworks.

03

Noise Control in High-Density Housing

Evidence-based strategies for managing traffic, construction, and mechanical noise in HDB estates and multi-family residential settings.

04

Acoustic Performance of Buildings

Room acoustics, speech intelligibility in schools and workplaces, and façade acoustic performance under tropical climate conditions.

05

Health & Wellbeing

Long-term impacts of noise exposure on cognitive performance, sleep quality, and mental health — with focus on schools, hospitals, and elderly facilities.

06

Computational & AI Methods

Machine learning for environmental sound classification, deep learning for acoustic scene analysis, and digital twins for acoustic simulation.
